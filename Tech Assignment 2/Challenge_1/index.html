ECE 140A: Summary of Week 2

  • Usually ideas get shut down before you even get to writing a single line of code.
  • Routing is like a switchboard operator
  • Planned Obsolescence - Companies that are aware that they will get replaced survive
  • Product market fit determines whether the business crosses the chasm.
  • The end-goal for businesses heavily determines the decisions the company makes in the present moment (Whether the company is made to last, or if it is looking to get bought)

Product Thinking Vocabulary

  • Minimum Viable Product - It is the smallest possible iterative experiment
  • RIBS Test (Relevant Inevitable Believable Sustainable/Scalable) - It is a way of testing whether a product may succeed. It evaluates it on such criteria.
  • Vitamin vs Painkiller - A vitamin is something supplemental that improves the quality of life of a customer. A painkiller is a solution to a problem, where that problem causes anguish to a customer.
